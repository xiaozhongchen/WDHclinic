The Canadian Dental Association (CDA) recommends that you get a dental exam and cleaning at least every six months. However, the frequency of your dental visits depends on several factors, including:

  • Oral health: If you're prone to gum disease or cavities, you may need to see your dentist more often. For example, people with gum disease often see their dentist every three to four months.
  • Age: Your age may be a factor in how often you should visit the dentist.
  • Risk factors: Other risk factors may also affect how often you should visit the dentist.
In addition to regular dental cleanings, you should also:
  • Brush your teeth at least twice a day or after every meal
  • Floss at least once a day or after every meal
  • Maintain good oral hygiene to keep germs under control
You should also talk to your dentist if you develop any new or concerning symptoms, such as: toothache, bleeding gums, and loose teeth.

The Canada Dental Care Plan (CDCP) is a federal initiative aimed at providing dental coverage to Canadians who lack access to private insurance. The eligibility criteria for the CDCP are outlined to help low- and middle-income families access dental care. Here's a breakdown of who is eligible:

Eligibility Criteria

1.Income Threshold:

Individuals or families with an annual income below a certain threshold. The threshold is typically adjusted for family size and economic conditions, with lower-income households prioritized.

2.Lack of Private Insurance:

  • Those who do not have access to private dental insurance.
  • Individuals covered under public programs may have partial eligibility, depending on overlap with provincial plans.

3.Age and Demographics:

  • Initial phases focus on children under 12 years old (as of 2022-2023).
  • Expansion to include teenagers under 18, seniors, and persons with disabilities is planned in subsequent phases.

4.Tax and Residency Requirements:

Must be a resident of Canada and have filed a recent income tax return to confirm income eligibility.

5.No Duplication with Provincial Plans:

The plan does not replace existing provincial dental coverage. It acts as a supplementary measure for services not covered by provincial or territorial programs.

What Is Covered?

The plan typically includes:
  • Basic dental services: cleanings, fillings, and x-rays.
  • Preventive care.
  • Emergency procedures.

How to Apply:

  • Applications are processed through the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).
  • Proof of income and lack of insurance may be required during the application process.
